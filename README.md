# DigiNode — Decentralized Digital Cancer Screening Network

A Digital Health Systems group project (HPI, University of Potsdam) proposing a decentralized cancer screening infrastructure for densely populated urban areas in Bangladesh, where only 4 public cancer hospitals exist — all located in Dhaka.

## The Problem

Bangladesh reported approximately 167,256 new cancer cases in 2022 (age-standardized incidence rate: 105.6 per 100,000). All major cancer treatment facilities are concentrated in Dhaka, forcing rural and underserved populations to travel long distances and face extended waiting periods — dramatically reducing early detection rates and survival outcomes.

## Our Solution

DigiNode proposes a **decentralized screening network** that brings cancer diagnostics to local hospitals across Bangladesh's regions, connected to a central data platform for specialist review and telemedicine consultation.

**Architecture:**

- Local hospitals conduct screenings and collect patient health records
- Records are pushed to a central database
- Central hospital specialists review cases remotely via telemedicine
- Patients receive follow-up care and referrals from their local hospital

## Target Cancer Types

Based on Bangladesh-specific epidemiological data:

- **Esophageal** (29.4% of cases)
- **Lip/Oral Cavity** (18.8%)
- **Lung** (15.3%)
- **Breast** (15.3% — leading cause in women)
- **Cervical** (30% of female cases)

## Value Chain

1. **Resource Management** — screening infrastructure setup in regional hospitals
2. **Screening Operations** — cancer screening + data collection
3. **Case Forwarding** — critical records transferred to specialist hospitals
4. **Community Outreach** — local awareness campaigns
5. **Follow-up Services** — personalized care and support

## Financial Model

**Year 1 total cost: €5,131,000**, primarily capital expenditure (screening equipment: €4.7M, data center: €86K).

**5-year projection:** Revenue grows from €4.5M (2025) to €6.6M (2029), with costs converging — break-even projected just beyond 2029.

**Revenue streams:** Pay-per-screening fees, government/NGO grants, anonymized data licensing.

## Tech Stack (proposed)

- **EHR platform** — cloud-based patient record system
- **Data center** — cloud storage, encrypted transmission, redundant backups
- **AI integration** — screening image analysis, risk stratification
- **Telemedicine** — specialist consultation platform
- **IT security** — firewalls, encryption, access control

## Key Recommendations

- Start with a **pilot program** using minimal equipment in 1-2 regional hospitals
- Prioritize **grant funding** from government and international health organizations
- Track metrics: screenings completed, detection rates, referral outcomes
